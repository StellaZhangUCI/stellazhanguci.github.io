About

Methodology & sources

This archive aspires to a single standard: every claim is anchored to a primary public source.

What this is

A public, citation-backed historical record of the UCI Paul Merage School of Business Stella Zhang New Venture Competition (originally the Merage Business Plan Competition, renamed in January 2022 following a naming gift from Stella Zhang FEMBA '05 and her spouse John Shen, extended in March 2025).

The site indexes all editions from the 2009 launch through the 2026 Grand Finale, with team rosters, prize amounts, project descriptions, judging panels (where preserved), and verified post-competition outcomes.

Verification standard

Every entry on this site cites a public source. Acceptable primary sources, in rough order of preference:

  1. Merage press releases (UCI's official record of the competition).
  2. UCI Samueli School of Engineering coverage.
  3. UCI Beall Applied Innovation profiles (innovation.uci.edu).
  4. UCI Beall + Butterworth competition results (bbcomp.tech.uci.edu).
  5. The company's own current website.
  6. Peer-reviewed publications, federal filings (FDA, SEC, SBIR), and patent records.
  7. Major business press coverage (TechCrunch, OC Business Journal, Business Wire).

LinkedIn is accepted for confirming individual roles but is flagged in citations because direct fetches are blocked by anti-bot protections.

What this is not

  • Not an official UCI page. This site is maintained independently and is not affiliated with UCI, the Paul Merage School of Business, or the Beall Center.
  • Not a complete record. Some pre-2017 editions are partial; rosters and prizes have been recovered where indexed UCI sources preserve them. Editions 2010, 2011, and 2015 remain structural gaps.
  • Not a ranking. Outcome categories (OPERATING, ACQUIRED, etc.) describe the current public state of a team, not a quality judgement.

Outcome categories

  • Operating — company is currently active with a live web presence, recent funding, or recent product activity.
  • Acquired — company was acquired (asset or equity); acquirer documented.
  • Pivoted — company changed direction substantially from the original NVC pitch; current product differs.
  • Shut down — company has formally wound down; site is parked or offline and no successor activity is visible.
  • Dormant — company has no recent public activity but has not formally announced shutdown.
  • No public outcome — researched but no traceable post-competition footprint surfaced.
