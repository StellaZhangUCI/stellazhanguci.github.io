People

Founders worth following

A short selection of NVC founders whose post-competition work has a traceable public footprint. All claims here are anchored to public profiles, press coverage or federal records.

2013 · Grand Prize

Sean White

Laser Associated Sciences. Built FlowMet, a continuous optical perfusion monitor. Sold the company to Medtronic in April 2020. LAS is the cleanest exit documented in the archive.

UCI Innovation coverage confirms the acquisition.

2017 · SoM Grand Prize

Leila Safavi

Purist. Operating in radiopharmaceutical isotope work via puristinc.com. Received over $223,000 in NIH SBIR funding in 2019 per UCI Beall coverage. Named to Oak Ridge National Lab's Innovation Crossroads Cohort 3.

Sources: UCI Beall SBIR coverage (April 2019). Founder LinkedIn.

2018 · Life Sciences 1st

Mahdi Tehrani and the BRIUS team

Mechanodontics to BRIUS Technologies. Rebranded after the competition. The Brava hidden-braces system launched commercially in June 2022. Closed a $19.5M Series B led by View Ventures in May 2025. Roughly $46.2M total raised per PitchBook.

Sources: bravabraces.com Series B page. PitchBook.

2018 · Consumer Services 1st

The Wing team

Wing to Wing Assistant. Rebranded as a managed virtual-assistant service. Raised a $2.1M seed in October 2021. Still operating in 2025 and 2026 per UCI Beall Innovation coverage.

Source: UCI Beall Innovation profile.

2020 · Grand Prize

Zarina Bahadur

123 Baby Box. MIE '20. Raised a $1.2M oversubscribed pre-seed in 2023 (XRC Labs, Sunstone, Salt Lake City Angels, California Crescent, plus angels Mark Friedman and Demos Parneros). Total raised has now grown past $1.3M per OCBJ. The strongest non-medical operating company in the archive.

Sources: Merage 2023-04 announcement. TechCrunch. OCBJ.

2020 · Consumer Services 1st

Jim Robertson, CJ Oveisi and Jit Malay

No Decaf Allowed to Moongoat. Subscription absorbed into Moongoat Coffee Roasters' programme in December 2022. Asset and subscriber transfer rather than equity. The third documented exit in the archive.

Source: nodecafallowed.com homepage transition note.

2022 · Business Products 1st

Catlin Tran

Nutripair. Business Economics '23. Received a $25,000 Verizon small-business grant in 2025 per UCI Social Sciences coverage. Currently an active member of the UCI Beall Wayfinder Startup cohort. Also placed 2nd at the 2021 Butterworth Product Development Competition.

Sources: UCI Social Sciences (March 2025). Wayfinder Startup directory.

2022 · Life Sciences 2nd

Sayenza Biosciences founders

Derek A. Banyard MD MS MBA, Jered B. Haun, Alan D. Widgerow. Clinical-stage adipose-tissue regenerative-medicine company. Raised approximately $1.16M. Joined the California Life Sciences FAST Spring 2024 cohort. Two peer-reviewed papers on the underlying fluidic platform published in 2025.

Source: UCI Beall (May 2021). sayenza.com.

2023 · Life Sciences 1st

Jennings Luu and Krzysztof Palczewski

Hyperion Therapeutics. Newport Beach. Seed-stage biopharma developing small-molecule SREDs for AMD, diabetic retinopathy and other retinal diseases. Received a $100,000 Proof-of-Product grant from UCI Beall Applied Innovation in 2023 per Irvine Standard.

Sources: hyperioneye.com. Irvine Standard (2025).

2025 · MIE + Consumer Services 1st

Ricianne "Chi" Rey

PCOSitive Buddy. Hired in October 2025 as the UCI ANTrepreneur Center Program Lead. Continues to be described as a co-founder of the PCOS wellness platform.

Source: UCI ANTrepreneur Center profile (January 2026).
