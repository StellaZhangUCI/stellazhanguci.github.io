The Stella Zhang New Venture Competition

A public, citation-backed record of seventeen editions of UCI's flagship student venture competition. Teams, prizes and what became of them.

This site indexes every Grand Prize, track award and specialty award disclosed in primary UCI Merage and UCI Samueli sources from 2009 onward. Every team has a per-year entry. Every post-competition outcome is anchored to a public source.

The record begins with the 2009 launch under the Paul Merage School of Business. It then follows the competition through the move to standard $10K and $5K track payouts in 2018. After that comes the January 2022 naming gift from Stella Zhang (FEMBA '05) and her spouse John Shen. Then the March 2025 $750,000 extension that pushed the prize pool past $150K.

Featured outcomes

A handful of teams have produced traceable post-competition trajectories. Each one is anchored to public filings, press coverage or a company announcement.

2018 · Life Sciences 1st

Mechanodontics to BRIUS Technologies

Rebranded after the competition and now based in Carrollton, TX. The Brava hidden-braces system launched commercially in June 2022. A $19.5M Series B led by View Ventures closed in May 2025. PitchBook reports roughly $46.2M in total funding to date.

2013 · Grand Prize

Laser Associated Sciences to Medtronic

Acquired by Medtronic in April 2020. The FlowMet continuous optical perfusion monitor was folded into Medtronic's peripheral-vascular portfolio. It is the cleanest exit documented in the archive.

2013 edition →

2020 · Grand Prize

123 Baby Box

Founded by Zarina Bahadur (MIE '20). Raised a $1.2M oversubscribed pre-seed in 2023 with XRC Labs, Sunstone, Salt Lake City Angels and California Crescent. Total raised has now grown past $1.3M. Revenue tripled in 2022 and the founder cites a roughly 14% monthly growth rate per OCBJ.

2018 · Consumer Services 1st

Wing to Wing Assistant

Rebranded after the competition as a managed virtual-assistant service. Raised a $2.1M seed in October 2021 and continues to operate in 2025 and 2026 per UCI Beall Innovation coverage.

2020 · Consumer Services 1st

No Decaf Allowed to Moongoat

The subscription was absorbed into Moongoat Coffee Roasters' own programme in December 2022. The terms were not disclosed but it was an asset and subscriber transfer rather than an equity deal. It is the third documented exit in the archive.

2017 · SoM Grand Prize

Purist

Founder Leila Safavi received $223K+ in NIH SBIR funding in 2019 per UCI Beall coverage. She was also named to Oak Ridge National Lab's Innovation Crossroads Cohort 3. Still operating in radiopharmaceutical isotope work as of last verification.

Timeline

  1. 2009. Inaugural Merage Business Plan Competition.
  2. 2013. Laser Associated Sciences wins the Grand Prize. Acquired by Medtronic in 2020.
  3. 2018. Standard track payouts reset to $10K and $5K. Mechanodontics (later BRIUS) wins Life Sciences.
  4. 2020. Pandemic-era $25K Grand Prize peak. Baby Box starts a streak of MIE-affiliated wins.
  5. January 2022. Stella Zhang (FEMBA '05) and John Shen announce the naming gift. The competition is renamed.
  6. March 2025. A $750,000 five-year extension pushes the prize pool past $150K.
  7. May 21, 2026. Twenty-second annual Grand Finale.
